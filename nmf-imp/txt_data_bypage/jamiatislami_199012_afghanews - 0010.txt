News
From The Battlefields
Herat Province
•The Mujahideen from Jami'at killed 24 militiamen belonging to the Haftabala group in Kohsan District on December 5. The Mujahideen also attacked a supply convoy of the Kabul regime on the Herat-Islam Qala Highway on December 4 killing 9 regime troops and destroying two vehicles.
•One officer and one soldier were killed and six soldiers and officers were captured when the Mujahideen attacked a regime security post near Herat airport bn November 20, HINA reported. The Mujahideen captured 40 light weapons, two cannons, two armored cars and three trucks.
•The Mujahideen captured 36 enemy soldiers and killed or wounded many of them when they attacked their post near the Herat Hotel on December 7. The Mujahideen seized 100 Kalashnikov	rifles,	two	heavy
Machine-guns	and	two	rocket
launchers.
Ghazni Province
One hundred eighty enemy soldiers and officers were killed and more than 300 sustained injuries when the Mujahideen attacked their positions in and around the city of Ghazni on November 24. The Mujahideen made use of ground-to-ground rockets and 82 mm guns and fired more than 500 shells on the enemy targets. The operation lasted one week. Many regime buildings were heavily damaged in the attack which was jointly launched by Jami'at and other Mujahideen groups. The Kabul regime has admitted the launching of the attack by the Mujahideen without giving any figures of the casualties among their troops.
Samangan Province
Eleven militiamen were killed by the Mujahideen and Dalkhaki village
was liberated on November 29. The enemy retaliated with heavy bombardments of the area with heavy weapons as a result of which many houses were destroyed and a number of animals were killed.
Kunduz Province
The Mujahideen from Jami'at cut the Qunduz-Sher Khan Bandar on November 27, HINA reported. The Mujahideen have captured Shahrawan town and Sher Khan port and fighting between the two sides continues.
The Kabul regime launched an attack to recapture Sher Khan port on December 9. Regime troops were supported by heavy tanks and armored cars. Thirty enemy soldiers and officers were killed and one armored car and ate truck were set on fire. The Mujahideen lost five men and 15 of them were wounded. Sher Khan Port is located on the Soviet border.
•The Mujahideen also have attacked Kunduz power plant and its security posts.
•A group of regime militiamen in Qala-i-Zal District defected to the Mujahideen from Jami'at on November 30 bringing with them 15 guns and one R-105 radio set. These militiamen and their families were posted in the security built around the center of Qala-i-Zal.
Kabul Province
•The Mujahideen from Jami'at have attacked regime security posts along the new section of the Salang road with heavy weapons, passing through Dehsabz District and Division No. 15 destroying one tank and killing at least two officer and five soldiers. The attack, launched from Koh-i-Safi, East of the capital, began on December 2 and continued for three days.
•One tank and three trucks were set on fire when the Mujahideen from Jami'at under the command of
Commander Mullah Ezat attacked the enemy positions in Arghandeh and Qargha with heavy weapons on December 8.
Jawzjan Province
•One truck was destroyed and 10 militiamen were killed when it hit a landmine, planted by the Mujahideen, on November 30 in Aqcha District.
•Two regime rival militia groups fought each other for two days using light and heavy weapons. Two armored car of the one militia group was destroyed by die rival group. Tension between the two groups is very high.
Baghlan Province
The Mujahideen launched a rocket attack on the enemy posts in Naqsha area of Baghlan on November 20, kill ing 12 enemy troops and wounding six of them, HINA reported. In retaliation the enemy air force bombed the villages controlled by the Mujahideen killing nine civilians.
Logar Province
•On December 4, the Mujahideen attacked the enemy position in Mohammad Agha district, destroying four tanks, one BM-41 and one BM-13 rocket launcher and set on fire an ammunition dump. Forty three soldiers and one officer woe killed and three soldiers defected. Three Mujahideen were wounded in the operation.
•On November 29, the Mujahideen captured seven posts in Mughul Khail and .Deh Naw of Mohammad Agha District and killed or wounded around 80 soldiers. Six Mujahideen were killed and 10 were wounded.
Faryab Province
The Mujahideen from Jami'at attacked the militia forces of the regime in Sherin Tagab District on November 19 as a result of which Sharab Big, a fit mous militia commander, was wounded and 400 armed militiamen were either captured by the Mujahideen or defected to diem.
On the same day, the Mujahideen attacked the enemy security posts near Maimana killing and wounding many regime troops. The Mujahideen captured 15 Kalashnikov rifles, three P.K Machine-guns, two ZPU guns (14.6 mm) and one mortar. Seven Mujahideen were killed and 15 were wounded in the operation.
The Kabul regime retaliated by aerial bombing of the villages con trolled by the Mujahideen killing 100 civilians.
